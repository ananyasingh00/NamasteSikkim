Chants & Rituals of Sikkim’s Monasteries

These sacred practices form the spiritual heartbeat of Sikkim’s monasteries. Each chant and ritual carries centuries of wisdom, devotion, and cultural identity.

Rumtek Monastery

Chant: Mahakala Puja

Ritual: Protector Deity Invocation

The Mahakala Puja is a fierce and powerful chant invoking Mahakala, the guardian of Dharma. Performed annually, it includes mask dances and offerings to dispel negativity and protect the spiritual path.

Pemayangtse Monastery

Chant: Guru Rinpoche Mantra

Ritual: Tsechu Festival Offerings

Celebrated with vibrant mask dances and sacred chants, the Tsechu Festival honors Guru Padmasambhava. The rituals are believed to cleanse karma and bring blessings to all attendees.

Tashiding Monastery

Chant: Bumchu Water Blessing

Ritual: Sacred Water Divination

The Bumchu ceremony involves opening a sealed vase of holy water to predict the fortunes of the year. Clear water signifies peace and prosperity, while murky water warns of challenges.
